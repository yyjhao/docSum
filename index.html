## Document Summarisation
## Motivation 1. Explosive number of documents online 1. Necessitates a fast way to filter the information 1. Automatic summarisation aids readers in such processes
## Examples: ![](images/search-engine.png) 1. Search engine previews 1. Automatic highlights
## Fomulation * Assumption 1. Articles consist of ideas 1. Ideas flow 1. Each sentence presents some ideas 1. Ideas are expressed through words 1. Overlap of words signifies relation of ideas 1. Thus relation of sentences * A sentence is *summarising* if ideas from many other sentences flow to it * A set of sentences with such characteristics can be a summary
## Fomulation * An article is a graph of sentences * Two sentences are connected if they are related * The weight of the edge is proportional to their relation * Compute how likely it is to flow to a certain sentence * Aggregate the top few sentences as summary
## Solution 1. The relation of two sentence, therefore the weight of edge connecting them * = number of word overlaps * An improvement will be to consider 1. Different word forms 1. Synonyms 1. Co-reference 1. The edges are bi-directional
## Solution 1. Simulate a random walk on such a graph * With PageRank™ 1. Rank the sentences with their PageRank™ score 1. Pick the top sentences
## Solution ### Potential problems 1. Repeated *summarising* sentences may be picked 2. Number of top sentences to be picked is unsure
